Farming Equipment - Subsoiler

What is a Subsoiler?

A subsoiler is an agricultural tool designed to break up compacted soil layers beneath the surface to improve soil structure, water infiltration, and root penetration. It is usually attached to a tractor and uses sharp, strong shanks to deeply penetrate the soil, improving its aeration and drainage.

Uses of a Subsoiler

  • Breaking Hardpan Layers: Loosens compacted soil layers to enhance root growth and improve water and air movement within the soil.
  • Improving Water Infiltration: Prevents surface runoff and allows rainwater or irrigation to penetrate deeper into the soil.
  • Enhancing Root Growth: Provides deeper penetration for roots, allowing crops to access nutrients and water from deeper layers.
  • Soil Aeration: Helps improve oxygen flow to the root zone by breaking up dense soil layers.
  • Improving Drainage: Helps reduce waterlogging by improving the soil’s ability to drain excess water.

Advantages of Using a Subsoiler

  • ✅ Improved Soil Structure: Loosens compacted soil, improving its ability to hold and retain nutrients and water.
  • ✅ Increased Crop Yields: Deeper, healthier roots lead to better crop growth and higher yields.
  • ✅ Enhanced Drainage: Prevents waterlogging and reduces soil erosion by improving the infiltration of water.
